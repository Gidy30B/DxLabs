# Asset Requirements

## Wardle

The current Wardle product section uses a conceptual learning-loop walkthrough because real product screenshots are not yet available. Future screenshots should show consistent sample-case content and must not expose personal information, private admin tools, editorial interfaces, user identifiers, institutional data or real patient details.

### Required Wardle Screenshots

1. Progressive clue screen
   - Recommended size: 1600 x 1000 px
   - Recommended aspect ratio: 16:10
   - Preferred format: WebP
   - Suggested filename: `wardle-progressive-clue.webp`
   - Content requirements: show history, examination or investigation evidence being progressively revealed.
   - Privacy requirements: anonymize all people, cases, institutions and account details.

2. Diagnosis submission screen
   - Recommended size: 1600 x 1000 px
   - Recommended aspect ratio: 16:10
   - Preferred format: WebP
   - Suggested filename: `wardle-diagnosis-entry.webp`
   - Content requirements: show the learner choosing or entering a likely diagnosis before explanation.
   - Privacy requirements: avoid private user progress, unpublished cases and internal authoring controls.

3. Case breakdown or educational feedback screen
   - Recommended size: 1600 x 1000 px
   - Recommended aspect ratio: 16:10
   - Preferred format: WebP
   - Suggested filename: `wardle-case-breakdown.webp`
   - Content requirements: show reasoning, key clues, differentials and practical learning points from the same sample case.
   - Privacy requirements: use only approved sample content and remove any identifiable user or patient information.

### Optional Wardle Video

Create a short optimized preview video to replace the current large demo asset later.

- Recommended length: 20-40 seconds
- Recommended size: 1280 x 720 px or 1600 x 900 px
- Recommended aspect ratio: 16:9
- Preferred format: MP4 with H.264 video and AAC audio, plus a WebM version if practical
- Suggested filename: `wardle-preview-demo.mp4`
- Content requirements: use the same sample case shown in the screenshots and demonstrate the interpret, commit and review loop.
- Privacy requirements: exclude real accounts, patient details, private admin screens and internal editorial interfaces.
