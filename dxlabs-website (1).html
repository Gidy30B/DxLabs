Why DxLabs

Healthcare's hardest gap isn't knowledge. It's the space between knowing and doing.

Every health system runs on the judgment of the people inside it — and that judgment is built slowly, unevenly, and often without enough support. Medical graduates leave school knowing the textbook and still spend years learning the instinct. Clinicians everywhere are asked to reason faster, under more pressure, with no support.

We started DxLabs because we lived that gap ourselves. Africa's health systems carry an outsized share of the world's disease burden with a fraction of its training infrastructure — which makes this the place to build tools that have to work well, not just look good. If we can make better clinical thinking a daily habit here, we can do it anywhere.

Mission

To build digital health products that empower healthcare professionals through better learning, smarter workflows, and accessible technology.

Vision

To become Africa's leading digital health innovation company — creating products that improve healthcare education, clinical practice, and patient outcomes worldwide.

Our products

Wardle is chapter one.

Our flagship product, built to prove a simple idea: clinical reasoning gets stronger when it's practiced daily, not just studied occasionally.

● Presented at MedEd Africa 2026

Wardle — Diagnose. Learn. Win.

A daily clinical reasoning game for medical students and junior doctors. One new case a day, six guesses, clues that unlock with every attempt — and a breakdown at the end that makes even a wrong answer worth something.

  • A new clinical case every day, six attempts to reach it
  • Progressive clues that mirror real diagnostic work-up
  • A shareable scorecard, built for the group chat
  • A post-game teaching breakdown on every case
